Structural Whitespace

The market lacks a scaled, purpose-built institution that combines integrated academics, sports science, structured residential life, and a girls-only safeguarding model.

PACE = the first institutional girls-only athlete development campus.
The switching barrier is trust + coordination — not "better coaching."

03 — The Problem

The Parent Problem

The current elite athlete development stack is fragmented: school + club + travel + private coaching + camps + ad hoc strength/medical support.

PACE does not create new spending — it consolidates fragmented spending into one accountable operator.

Coordination Failure

  • Schedule conflicts
  • Double training loads

Logistics Burden

  • Constant travel
  • Multiple coaches
  • Academic tradeoffs

Outcome Uncertainty

  • Unclear recruiting path
  • Inconsistent development
  • Injury & burnout risk

Misaligned Incentives

  • Clubs optimize for team
  • Families optimize for individual + college

High spend ≠ integrated outcomes. Parents seek a coherent, supervised, measurable pathway.

25 — Risk Framework

Key Risks and Mitigations

We've modeled the downside. Every risk below has a structural mitigation embedded in the operating model.

Risk / Mitigation Matrix
RiskSeverityMitigation
Enrollment ramp slower than modeled Medium Staffing is step-function (scales with enrollment bands, not linear); day-student mix absorbs boarding shortfall; camps/clinics generate revenue during ramp; $8.73M working capital buffer sized for 12+ months of runway beyond projection
Construction cost overrun Medium 10% contingency ($2.96M) built into OpCo raise; 10% CapEx overrun = ~$5.78M funded 50/50 equity/debt; rent floor re-bases via DSCR; Derrick Evers has $5B+ in development experience across similar asset classes
Interest rate increase Medium Model stress-tested at 7%, 8%, 9% — OpCo remains EBITDA positive post-rent in all scenarios; DSCR rent floors mechanically derived; post-stabilization refinancing provides optionality
Safeguarding incident High Impact Girls-only campus is the safeguarding architecture; independent committee with broad oversight; external audits; incident governance protocols; residential house structure with defined supervision ratios; this is the moat, not an afterthought
Discount drift eroding tuition Low Scholarship governance treats discounts as capital allocation; step-up freeze trigger at >15% trailing discount; donor-funded foundation preserves tuition integrity; golf modeled at full-pay (0%)
Key person dependency Low Three founding principals span sports (Jones), operations (Chaney), and real estate (Evers); advisory architecture covers all sport-specific and compliance functions; early senior hires de-risk operational concentration
