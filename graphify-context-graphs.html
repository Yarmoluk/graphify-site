The Two Clocks

Neo4j captures the event clock. Graphify.md architects the structure clock.

Neo4j's own framing describes two clocks every organization runs on: the "state clock" (what's true right now) and the "event clock" (what happened and why). There's a third clock they don't name — the structure clock: how domain knowledge, relationships, and constraints are organized in the first place.

01

State Clock

What's true right now. Customer's credit limit, inventory count, account status. Traditional databases handle this. It's solved.

02

Event Clock

What happened and why. Decision traces, reasoning memory, audit trails. This is Neo4j's context graph thesis — and it's a real gap.

03

Structure Clock

How domain knowledge is organized. Entities, relationships, constraints, decision logic. This is what Graphify.md builds — the architecture that makes both other clocks useful.

Without domain structure, decision traces are just logs. Without decision traces, domain structure is static. The complete context graph needs both.
