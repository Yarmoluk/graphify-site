The Lesson From India

HTEC GROUP
What happened — and what it means for Galway.
John Braunfeld — Medtronic
"We failed in India because we just hired people. We hired people, hired people, hired people — and we didn't invest the time to train them."

India: What Went Wrong

  • Engineers hired without structured onboarding
  • No domain-specific training (FDA, CE Mark, ISO 13485)
  • Assumed productivity without a ramp plan
  • High churn — engineers left as they found better offers
  • Result: Center underperformed. Investment wasted.

    Galway: What HTEC Does Differently

    • 52-week structured onboarding curriculum
    • Medical device domain training from Day 1 (FDA, CE, MDR)
    • Embedded Medtronic architects (1 per 10 engineers)
    • Phased reviews at months 3, 6, 10 — underperformers released early
    • Career pathways to anchor talent post-transfer
