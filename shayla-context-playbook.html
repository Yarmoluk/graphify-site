Conversations That Close

When they say: "We're evaluating AI models"

The Model Selection Trap

Their pain: Months spent benchmarking GPT vs Claude vs Gemini. Committees, POCs, vendor demos. Still no clear winner.
Your bridge: "The models are converging — top 4 are within 5% on major benchmarks. The organizations pulling ahead stopped debating which model and started asking: how structured is the context feeding it? Same model, structured context vs unstructured — that's a 170x difference in reasoning density. The hire that changes the game isn't an ML engineer to evaluate models. It's a context architect who structures your domain knowledge so any model performs."
Close: "I can help you find that person — or build that capability on your existing team."
When they say: "Our AI outputs aren't trustworthy"

The Hallucination Problem

Their pain: AI gives confident wrong answers. Leadership doesn't trust it. Adoption stalls.
Your bridge: "Hallucination isn't a model problem — it's a context problem. When you feed an LLM unstructured text and hope it figures out the relationships, it guesses. When you give it a knowledge graph where entities, dependencies, and provenance are explicit, it reasons. The model gets it right on the first pass because the relationships are already there. Your team needs people who can structure domain knowledge — that's the fix."
Close: "The talent profile for this is different from what you're posting for. Let me show you what a context architecture hire looks like."
When they say: "We built a RAG pipeline but it's not working"

The RAG Plateau

Their pain: Invested in vector databases, embedding pipelines, retrieval tuning. Results are mediocre. Retrieves the wrong chunks. Misses connections.
Your bridge: "RAG retrieves fragments. It doesn't understand relationships. If you structure the knowledge into a graph before it hits the retrieval pipeline — entities connected, hierarchies preserved — RAG gets dramatically more precise. The preprocessing layer is what's missing. And the people who build that layer think differently from your current ML team. They think in domains and relationships, not just embeddings and vectors."
Close: "This is the capability gap I'm seeing across healthcare and finance right now. The teams that add a context architect see their existing RAG investment start working."
When they say: "We're worried about vendor lock-in"

The Sovereignty Play

Their pain: Proprietary embeddings, vendor-specific vector stores, platform-dependent AI features. Switching costs grow every month.
Your bridge: "The smartest organizations are building their knowledge layer in plain text — structured markdown that works in any model, any platform. No vector DB to migrate. No embeddings to re-encode. The intelligence is in the structure, not the vendor. That's a strategic decision that needs someone who understands both the domain and the portability architecture."
Close: "I can help you find leaders who build sovereign AI infrastructure — knowledge that your organization owns and moves freely."
