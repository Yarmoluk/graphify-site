7 actionable roll-up theses extracted from the Graphify knowledge graph. Each one is a $500M+ play.

Roll-Up #1: Contact Center AI

Consolidation Play — $800M+ Addressable

12 companies, $2.1B deployed, doing the same thing. Acquire 3-4 sub-scale players (Forethought, Crescendo, PolyAI, Cognigy) at reasonable multiples, consolidate tech stack, cross-sell enterprise accounts. Platform play against Sierra and Parloa.

Forethought $115M Crescendo $50M PolyAI $86M Cognigy Retell

Roll-Up #2: AI SDR Stack

Consolidation Play — $200M+ Addressable

6 companies with identical "AI sales agent" pitch. None has escaped velocity. Acquirer rolls up 3-4 (Monaco, Actively, Winn.AI, FINNY), combines prospecting + guidance + engagement into one platform. Challenge Rox before they earn their $1.2B valuation.

Monaco $35M Actively AI $22.5M Winn.AI $18M FINNY $17M 1mind $40M

Roll-Up #3: AI Recruiting

Consolidation Play — $150M+ Addressable

5 companies doing "AI recruiter." Mercor leads but hasn't locked the market. Acquire Perfect, Alex, Tezi — combine sourcing + screening + placement into one end-to-end agent. Recruiting is a $500B TAM with zero loyalty.

Perfect $23M Alex $20M Tezi $9M Paraform $65M

Roll-Up #4: Healthcare Agent Stack

Vertical Integration — $300M+ Addressable

Abridge owns ambient scribe. But scheduling, rev cycle, triage, and enrollment are fragmented across 4+ small players. Roll up Tennr + Doctronic + Attuned + Atlas Health into one "healthcare ops agent platform." Sell to health systems as integrated suite vs. point solutions.

Tennr $101M Doctronic $65M Attuned $13M Atlas Health

Roll-Up #5: Finance Back-Office

Consolidation Play — $400M+ Addressable

AppZen (auditing), LayerX (expense), Rogo (workflows), Unique AI (compliance). All touching the same CFO buyer. One platform that does all four beats four logins. AppZen is the natural anchor; acquire 2-3 complementary pieces.

LayerX $140M Rogo $75M Unique AI $30M Data Sutram $9M

Roll-Up #6: Agentic Security

Strategic M&A Target — $500M+ Acquirable

$515M deployed across 5 security agent startups in 12 months. CrowdStrike, Palo Alto, or SentinelOne will acquire 1-2 within 18 months. 7AI vs Kai is a coinflip — the other gets rolled up. Noma and Upwind protect the agents themselves (meta-play).

7AI $130M Kai $125M Noma $100M Upwind $100M Zafran $60M

Roll-Up #7: Agent Infrastructure

Platform Play — $200M+ Addressable

Too many "agent builder" platforms with no differentiation. Relevance AI, Dust, Trace, Arcade — all competing for the same "build your own agent team" buyer. The infrastructure layer will consolidate around LangChain + 1-2 others. Rest are acqui-hire fodder.

Relevance AI $64.6M Dust $21.5M Arcade $12M Trace $3M
