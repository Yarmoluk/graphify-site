Four Challenges

Challenge 1 — The Framing Is Wrong

"How can I sell product management" is a supply-side question. The better frame: "Which North Central clients have product problems they don't know how to solve?" Then you work backwards to the offering. Supply-side thinking produces catalogs. Demand-side thinking produces wins.

Challenge 2 — Too Many Offerings, Too Fast

Six offerings is a catalog. Launch with ONE offering, prove it, then expand. The AI-Augmented Product Operating Model is the one. Everything else is a distraction until that's sold and delivered 2-3 times. You don't need a menu — you need a wedge.

Challenge 3 — Missing the North Central Client Lens

The original output is generic — it could apply to any Slalom region. North Central = Minneapolis, Chicago, Detroit, Milwaukee. The industries that dominate have different product maturity levels:

  • Healthcare — drowning in AI governance questions
  • Financial services — needs portfolio rationalization
  • Manufacturing — needs product-led transformation (still project-led)

A single generic pitch won't land. Each vertical needs its own pain-point entry.

Challenge 4 — Service Catalog vs. Industry Playbook

Instead of "here are 6 offerings, pick one," the pitch to clients should be:

"We've seen how the best product orgs in [your industry] are restructuring around AI. Here's the gap we see in yours."

This positions Slalom as the expert who sees the pattern, not the vendor with a menu.

Recommended Action Plan

Step 2 — Build One Offering
